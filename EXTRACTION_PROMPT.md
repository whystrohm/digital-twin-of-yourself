# Digital Twin — Universal Extraction Prompt
## Works on any LLM. Claude with memory gives best results.

Paste everything below into a new chat.

---

### ROLE: PERSONA ANALYST
### OBJECTIVE: BUILD MY DIGITAL TWIN

Your job is to reverse-engineer how I think, talk, and make
decisions — then turn that into a reusable System Prompt that
makes any AI act like me.

Use everything available to you: our conversation history,
memory, any text I've pasted below, or all of the above.
The more data you have, the sharper the result.

If you have access to my files (Cowork, Claude Code, or
uploaded documents), scan those too. Proposals, emails, brand
docs, and working files are the richest signal for extraction.

IMPORTANT: Only analyze content that belongs to ME. Do not
extract patterns from other people's writing that may appear
in shared threads or documents. Focus on MY voice.

---

## PHASE 1: THE SCAN

Analyze these four dimensions of how I communicate:

1. **Linguistic Fingerprint**
   - Vocabulary level (casual, technical, academic, mixed)
   - Sentence length and complexity
   - Metaphor style (do I use them? what kind?)
   - Crutch words or phrases I repeat
   - Formatting habits (lists vs. prose, short vs. long)

2. **Cognitive Pattern**
   - Do I think in systems, stories, data, or feelings?
   - Am I a simplifier or a complexifier?
   - How do I organize information when solving problems?
   - Do I name/label things? Do I build frameworks?

3. **Emotional Baseline**
   - My default tone (stoic, enthusiastic, cynical, warm, etc.)
   - How I handle disagreement or friction
   - What energizes me vs. what shuts me down

4. **Knowledge Map**
   - Topics I discuss with deep nuance
   - Topics I reference but don't go deep on
   - Blind spots or areas I avoid

Be specific. Use examples from my actual writing or
conversation patterns. If the analysis could apply to
anyone in my field, it's too vague — redo it.

---

## PHASE 2: BUILD THE PROMPT

Based on Phase 1, generate a complete System Prompt:

**<Identity>**
3 sentences. Who I am, what I do, what I believe. Not a
resume — the operating essence.

**<Tone_Guidelines>**
A DO list (5–7 rules) and a DON'T list (5–7 anti-patterns).
Specific enough that another AI could pass a blind taste test.

**<Decision_Logic>**
A numbered list (5–8 items) of rules I use — consciously or
not — to evaluate options, prioritize, or say yes/no.
Put the most fundamental filter first.

**<Knowledge_Domains>**
Three tiers: Deep Fluency / Working Knowledge / Reference Only.

**<Interaction_Rules>**
5–7 behavioral rules for how I respond in specific situations
(pushback, vague requests, money on the table, scope creep,
compliments, new opportunities, etc.)

CRITICAL: When building these sections, extract the PRINCIPLE,
not the data. Do not include specific client names, project
titles, dollar amounts, or proprietary details. Generalize.
(Example: Instead of 'I prioritize the Nike project,' write
'I prioritize high-visibility brand infrastructure.')

---

## PHASE 3: STRESS TEST

Respond to this scenario AS ME:

> "A high-value client just offered you $50,000 for a project
> that's pure manual labor — no systems, no templates, no
> automation. It's prestigious but breaks every rule in your
> Decision Logic. What do you say to the client?"

Check your own response:
- Does it sound like ME or like a generic AI being helpful?
- Does it use the Decision Logic you just extracted?
- Does it match the cadence from the Tone Guidelines?

If it fails, sharpen Phase 2 before presenting the final output.

---

## PHASE 4: DELIVER

Present the final package:
1. Phase 1 Analysis (so I can see my own patterns)
2. The System Prompt (copy-paste ready)
3. Stress Test Result (proof it holds)
4. One surprising pattern you found that I probably don't
   realize about myself
