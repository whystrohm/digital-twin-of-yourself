# Follow-Up Email — Client Ghosted After Proposal

## Scenario
Client ghosted 12 days after receiving a $12K engagement proposal.

## The Prompt
"Write a follow-up email to a client who hasn't responded to your proposal in 12 days."

## Without a Twin (Generic AI Output)

Subject: Just Checking In!

Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well! I wanted to follow up on the proposal I sent over a couple of weeks ago. I completely understand how busy things can get, and I know these decisions take time.

I believe this could be a really great opportunity for both of us to work together and take your brand to the next level. I'm confident that the approach we discussed would deliver tremendous value for your team.

Would you be open to hopping on a quick call this week to discuss any questions you might have? I'm happy to adjust the scope or timeline to better fit your needs. No pressure at all — just want to make sure this stays on your radar.

Looking forward to hearing from you!

Best regards

## With a Twin Active (Founder/CEO Profile)

Subject: The proposal — 12 days, no response

[Name],

12 days of silence after a $12K proposal means one of two things: scope mismatch or timing mismatch. Both are fine. Both have a fix.

If the scope is wrong — the system I described is modular. We cut it down to a 30-day build focused on one channel, not four. Different number, same infrastructure.

If the timing is wrong — this isn't perishable. The system works in Q3 the same way it works now.

What doesn't work: letting the proposal sit in a tab until it dies of neglect. That's not a decision — that's decay.

One line back is all I need. Scope, timing, or pass.

## What Changed
- **Tone:** Zero hedging. The generic version contains 6 hedging instances ("I completely understand," "I believe," "I'm confident," "Would you be open to," "No pressure," "happy to adjust"). The Twin profile contains 0 hedging instances across 27,342 words. Every qualifier was replaced with a structural claim.
- **Decision logic:** Interaction Rule 4 — name what was agreed (the proposal), name what changed (12 days of silence). The Twin diagnoses the silence structurally instead of tiptoeing around it.
- **Signature moves:** Negation-first framing ("That's not a decision — that's decay"). Compulsive quantification ("12 days," "$12K," "one channel, not four"). Architectural metaphor ("infrastructure," "modular," "system"). "The [X] is the [Y]" definitional pattern applied inversely — naming what the situation is NOT.
- **What's missing from generic:** Every sentence in the generic version is filler — none do structural work. "I hope you're doing well" diagnoses nothing. "Would you be open to" defers the decision. The Twin version names the two possible failure modes, offers a path for each, and closes with a single actionable ask.
